Problem Set: Congruence, Binary, Recurrence, and Induction

1. Congruence and Binary Representation

(a) For each of the following, compute the value and convert your answer to binary:

  • \( 7 \bmod 2 \)
  • \( 7 \bmod 4 \)
  • \( 7 \bmod 8 \)

(b) True or false: For \( n \in \mathbb{N} \), \( n \) is divisible by 8 if and only if, when written in binary, its rightmost three bits are 000. Justify your answer.

(c) Convert \( 1101101_2 \) to decimal, then reduce the result modulo 5.

2. Congruence Classes

Let \( B = \{ x \in \mathbb{Z} \mid x \equiv 2 \pmod{7} \} \).

(a) List 2 positive and 2 negative elements of \( B \).

(b) Are all elements of \( B \) also congruent modulo 14? Explain why or give a counterexample.

3. Recurrence Relations

Consider the recurrence relation \( b_n = 4b_{n-1} - 3b_{n-2} \), with initial conditions \( b_0 = 1 \), \( b_1 = 2 \).

(a) Compute \( b_2 \), \( b_3 \), and \( b_4 \).

(b) Find the characteristic equation and its roots.

(c) Solve for constants using initial conditions to find a closed-form expression for \( b_n \).

4. Divisibility Proof by Induction

Prove by induction that for all integers \( n \geq 0 \):

\[ 11^{n} - 4^{n} \text{ is divisible by } 7. \]

Answer Key

1. Congruence and Binary

(a)

  • \( 7 \bmod 2 = 1 \) → binary \( 1_2 \)
  • \( 7 \bmod 4 = 3 \) → binary \( 11_2 \)
  • \( 7 \bmod 8 = 7 \) → binary \( 111_2 \)

(b) True. Justification: The last three bits represent the value modulo 8. If they are 000, the number is a multiple of 8.

(c)

\( 1101101_2 = 1\cdot 2^6 + 1\cdot 2^5 + 0\cdot 2^4 + 1\cdot 2^3 + 1\cdot 2^2 + 0\cdot 2^1 + 1\cdot 2^0 \)

\( = 64 + 32 + 0 + 8 + 4 + 0 + 1 = 109 \)

\( 109 \bmod 5 = 4 \)

2. Congruence Classes

(a) Positive: \( 2, 9 \)
Negative: \( -5, -12 \)

(b) No. Counterexample: \( 2 \) and \( 9 \) are both in \( B \), but \( 2 \not\equiv 9 \pmod{14} \).

3. Recurrence Relations

(a)

\( b_2 = 4\cdot 2 - 3\cdot 1 = 5 \)

\( b_3 = 4\cdot 5 - 3\cdot 2 = 14 \)

\( b_4 = 4\cdot 14 - 3\cdot 5 = 41 \)

(b) Characteristic equation: \( r^2 - 4r + 3 = 0 \) → \( (r-1)(r-3) = 0 \) → \( r = 1, 3 \)
General solution: \( b_n = A\cdot 1^n + B\cdot 3^n = A + B\cdot 3^n \)

(c) Using \( b_0 = 1 \): \( A + B = 1 \)
Using \( b_1 = 2 \): \( A + 3B = 2 \)
Subtract: \( 2B = 1 \) → \( B = \frac12 \), \( A = \frac12 \)
So \( b_n = \frac12 (1 + 3^n) \)

4. Divisibility Proof by Induction

Base case (\( n=0 \)): \( 11^0 - 4^0 = 1 - 1 = 0 \), divisible by 7.

Inductive step:
Assume \( 11^k - 4^k = 7m \) for some integer \( m \).
For \( n = k+1 \):
\( 11^{k+1} - 4^{k+1} = 11\cdot 11^k - 4\cdot 4^k \)
\( = 11\cdot 11^k - 11\cdot 4^k + 11\cdot 4^k - 4\cdot 4^k \)
\( = 11(11^k - 4^k) + (11 - 4)\cdot 4^k \)
\( = 11(7m) + 7\cdot 4^k \)
\( = 7(11m + 4^k) \)
Divisible by 7.

By induction, true for all \( n \geq 0 \).
